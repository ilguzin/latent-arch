Software engineering is living through its deepest transformation in decades. Models write code, agents carry whole workflows, and the limit on what gets built is shifting from how fast we can type to how well we can think — how clearly we frame problems, draw boundaries, and judge results. Careers built on the scarcity of hands are giving way to careers built on clarity of intent.

Latent Arch is a blog about the craft of building software when AI agents do most of the typing. The central thread is the shift from writing code to orchestrating it — what it takes to become an *agentic solo engineer*: one person who, through agents, delivers the output of a team without lowering the bar on architecture, quality, or judgment.

Expect field notes rather than hot takes: working practices, honest assessments of what agents can and cannot carry, and the engineering habits that survive the shift. The longer arguments live in the [blog](/posts/); [AI News](/ai-news/) is a daily model-curated selection of releases and research worth reading.
